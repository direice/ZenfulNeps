Council Meeting
This is Green Trussville, a site to keep everyone informed about the property on Highway 11 across from Winn Dixie in Trussville, AL. A developer has asked for the property to be rezoned from residential to C2 commercial and RG residential garden homes.

Our issue on the zoning matter at 522 and 518 Main Street has been taken off the agenda for the City Council Meeting on May 14th. The Developers have to go back to the Planning and Zoning Board. More info to come!!!

This video shows the beautiful wooded area between Birch Street and Dew Drive that will be destroyed if the rezoning happens. Across the highway you can see the Winn Dixie plaza. Leave the Green, Trussville!

The development plan shown below for the property across from Winn Dixie includes a gas/service station, a fast food restaurant, other businesses yet to be determined and FORTY zero-lot line houses. We do NOT believe this proposal to be in the best interest of our city. Some of the reasons are listed below:
  • Traffic is already out of control on Highway 11
  • Potential flooding from improperly controlled water runoff
  • Destruction of natural area and wildlife habitat
  • Reduced quality of life, especially for residents of Dew Gardens
  • Inconsistent with the Trussville Comprehensive Plan to have a green and walkable downtown district
  • Best use of property is a park/gateway to Downtown Trussville

The Conceptual Design Plans

There was a meeting of the Trussville Planning and Zoning Board on March 11 at which the board voted to recommend to our City Council that they NOT approve the zoning change. However, it is important to note it is only a RECOMMENDATION! The City Council can decide to ignore the Planning and Zoning Board's recommendation. That is why we must continue to show our opposition towards this development.
