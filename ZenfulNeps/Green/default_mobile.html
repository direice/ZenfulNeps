Council Meeting
This is Green Trussville, a site to keep everyone informed about the property on Highway 11 across from Winn Dixie in Trussville, AL. A developer has asked for the property to be rezoned from residential to C2 commercial.

Save the date! June 10th. The request goes back to the Trussville Planning & Zoning Board.
BE THERE! WE HAVE SO MANY QUESTIONS!!

  • Hambone Properties seems to have dropped their rezoning request.
  • Highpointe Properties and The City of Trussville have now made a rezoning request.
  • The new rezoning request is for C2 (Commercial) for the entire property.
  • This raises more questions than answers.
    • Why does a park, trail, and Board of Education building need a C2 (Commercial) Rezoning? Why not IN-2 (Institutional) and P (Park District)?
    • What guarantees that there will be a "Community Park" and "Walking Trail"?
    • Who maintains this new "Community Park" and "Walking Trail"?
    • Can the land be divided and sold by the developer to other developers for commercial development after being rezoned?
    • What is a "Tree Safe Area"? How many mature trees won't be saved?
    • What will become of the lots next to the School Board and Gas Station?
  • IT IS MORE IMPORTANT THAN EVER TO STAY ON TOP OF THIS ISSUE.
  • The easiest way to share your thoughts on the new proposal is on the Facebook Group called STOP DEVELOPMENT ACROSS FROM WINN DIXIE. What questions do you have?
This issue is still very much up in the air and we need your support and involvement now more than ever.

The New Conceptual Design Plans
This video shows the beautiful wooded area between Birch Street and Dew Drive that will be destroyed if the rezoning happens. Across the highway you can see the Winn Dixie plaza. Leave the Green, Trussville!

The development for the property across from Winn Dixie includes a gas/service station, and other businesses yet to be determined. We do NOT believe this proposal to be in the best interest of our city.

There was a meeting of the Trussville Planning and Zoning Board on March 11 at which the board voted to recommend to our City Council that they NOT approve the previous zoning change request.
